## Thesis-Backed Invariant Audit

### Section `8.3.1 Inlining bounds`

The thesis states that inlining is allowed only when inverse translation can
rebuild the bound unambiguously, when the node is not `usedtwice`, when the
binder is the structural ancestor, and when the binding flag can be recovered
from variance.

Repo-local audit obligations for the selected `baseTarget` subject:

- alias-bound ownership must identify which bound is being inlined and which
  binder still owns it after translation;
- any future `N4` target must fail closed if the candidate proof depends on a
  shared-use node, a non-ancestor binder, or an unreconstructible
  binding-flag reconstruction story; and
- the admissibility story must be phrased as inverse-translation
  reconstructibility, not as "the implementation probably knows what we meant."

### Section `15.6.2 Expressivity of alias bounds`

The thesis says special care is required for alias bounds in xMLF, that inert
or alias bounds may need full inlining, and that the graphic presentation does
not directly express alias bounds.

Repo-local audit obligations for the selected `baseTarget` subject:

- alias-bound ownership must stay explicit enough that a later bounded slice
  can say whether it is preserving, inlining, or rejecting an alias-bound
  presentation;
- any future `N4` target must keep alias-bound handling local to the selected
  `baseTarget` route rather than widening into another fallback family or a
  solver-wide search; and
- if the exact future slice cannot explain alias-bound behavior without
  implicit or hidden translation steps, the slice is not admissible.

### Section `17.3 Perspectives`

The thesis says recursive types plus second-order polymorphism are already
tricky, cyclic term-graphs were not the explored route, and the likely main
difficulty lies in recursion in the binding structure.

Repo-local audit obligations for the selected `baseTarget` subject:

- recursive planning must remain binder-centric and explicit-only;
- the selected subject must not rely on equi-recursive equality, implicit
  unfolding, or cyclic term-graph assumptions; and
- any later exact target must keep the reasoning structurally acyclic and
  narrowly tied to binding-structure reconstruction rather than solver-wide
  recursive inference.
