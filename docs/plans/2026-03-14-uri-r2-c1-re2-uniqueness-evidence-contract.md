## Contract Subject

The bounded subject for uniqueness is exactly the already-bounded local root or one equivalent local cluster inside `URI-R2-C1` that `R2` admitted and that `RE1` requires to be provenance-stable.

That same bounded subject remains the only admissible comparison domain for `RE2`.

Required subject properties:

- the subject is finite and local to `URI-R2-C1`;
- the subject is already inside one `single-SCC` and one `single-binder-family`;
- the subject does not expand into multiple local roots, multiple local clusters, or a widened comparison class;
- uniqueness is judged only among admissible bounded interpretations already inside that one subject boundary;
- no outside root, outside cluster, widened owner family, replay-domain repair, or non-local search result may enter the comparison set.

Equivalent local cluster means only a bounded cluster whose members are already part of the same local admissible subject and do not require cluster salvage, post hoc assembly, or widened search to be treated as one subject.

## Admissible Evidence

Later review may count evidence as uniqueness evidence for `URI-R3-O5` only if it satisfies all of the following reviewer-checkable requirements.

### 1. One fixed admissible subject only

The evidence must identify one bounded local root or one bounded equivalent local cluster already admitted inside `URI-R2-C1`.

Admissible evidence must show:

- the subject is the same bounded subject already required by `RE1`;
- the subject is already local rather than discovered by widened comparison;
- the subject is singular rather than one choice among live competitors; and
- the subject can be named without introducing any new candidate class outside the fixed boundary.

### 2. Uniqueness by exclusion inside the fixed boundary

The evidence must show that exactly one admissible bounded interpretation survives inside the fixed subject boundary.

Admissible evidence must show:

- every bounded interpretation that remains inside the same local subject boundary collapses to the same root or equivalent local cluster;
- no second admissible local root remains live;
- no second admissible local cluster remains live; and
- the uniqueness argument works by exclusion of inadmissible alternatives rather than by ranking or preference among multiple live candidates.

### 3. No heuristic or solver-choice dependence

The evidence must show that uniqueness does not depend on heuristic ranking, tie-breaking, or solver-choice-dependent comparison.

Admissible evidence must show:

- no heuristic ranking is needed to prefer one bounded candidate over another;
- no tie-breaking rule is needed to choose among locally live roots or clusters;
- no solver-order, replay-order, or reconstruction-order choice changes which subject is admissible; and
- no success claim depends on a preference policy that is weaker than exact bounded exclusion.

### 4. No widened comparison, repair, or salvage

The evidence must show that uniqueness is decided without broadening the active subject or repairing ambiguity after the fact.

Admissible evidence must show:

- no competing roots are compared outside the one bounded local subject;
- no competing clusters are compared outside the one bounded local subject;
- no multi-cluster or multi-SCC salvage is needed to recover uniqueness;
- no widened search, non-local propagation, replay-domain widening, or owner repair is needed; and
- no provenance repair is needed after `RE1` to manufacture uniqueness.

### 5. Consistency with inherited invariant boundaries

The evidence must remain consistent with the authoritative inherited invariant audit and the accepted `RE1` provenance contract.

Admissible evidence must show:

- principality risk remains fail-closed if more than one admissible interpretation stays live;
- occurs-check and termination boundaries are not weakened to obtain uniqueness;
- reconstruction, reification, and witness replay still consume one bounded subject only;
- binding-tree discipline remains single-family and deterministic for the claimed subject; and
- the uniqueness claim does not rely on semantics or search behavior that the inherited audit already rejects.

## Reviewer Gate

A later reviewer may mark `URI-R3-O5` cleared only by pointing to evidence that demonstrates all of the following at once:

- one bounded local root or one bounded equivalent local cluster inside `URI-R2-C1`;
- that same subject is already authoritative under `RE1` and remains the only admissible comparison domain;
- exactly one admissible bounded interpretation survives inside that fixed domain;
- no second live local root, no second live local cluster, and no second solver-choice-dependent recursive shape remains admissible;
- uniqueness is established by bounded exclusion only, without heuristic ranking, tie-breaking, widened comparison, or repair;
- the claim stays inside the inherited principality, replay, ownership, occurs-check, termination, and acyclicity boundaries.

A later reviewer must keep `URI-R3-O5` uncleared if any of the following findings appear:

- more than one live local root remains admissible;
- more than one live local cluster remains admissible;
- uniqueness appears only after heuristic ranking, tie-breaking, or solver-choice preference;
- uniqueness appears only after widened search, non-local propagation, replay-domain widening, owner repair, or provenance repair;
- uniqueness depends on comparing multiple clusters, multiple SCCs, or multiple binder families;
- the claim depends on prototype evidence, experiment results, or implementation drift rather than inherited bounded authority.
