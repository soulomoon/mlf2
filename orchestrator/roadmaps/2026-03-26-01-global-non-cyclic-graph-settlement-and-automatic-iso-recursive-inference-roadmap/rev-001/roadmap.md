# Global Non-Cyclic-Graph Settlement And Automatic Iso-Recursive Inference Roadmap

## Context

- This roadmap revision succeeds the completed same-lane retained-child
  public-output continuity vs `non-cyclic-graph` successor control plane
  whose accepted execution record ended at `orchestrator/rounds/round-098`.
- The live controller resolves this roadmap revision through
  `orchestrator/state.json` fields `roadmap_id`, `roadmap_revision`, and
  `roadmap_dir`; the top-level `orchestrator/roadmap.md`,
  `orchestrator/retry-subloop.md`, and `orchestrator/verification.md` files
  are pointer stubs only.
- Completed rounds `round-001` through `round-098` remain authoritative
  historical evidence. They are predecessor evidence for this refreshed
  control plane and must not be reinterpreted into broader capability.
- The inherited baseline contract remains unchanged: explicit recursive
  annotations are the production baseline, recursive meaning remains
  iso-recursive only, and the explicit-only / non-equi-recursive /
  non-cyclic-graph / no-second-interface / no-fallback boundary remains live
  unless an accepted roadmap decision explicitly changes it.
- The accepted round-098 decision fixed the strongest lawful exact-pocket
  result as `blocker debt remains within the current architecture`, not
  `reopen the non-cyclic-graph revision question`, for one same-lane
  retained-child public-output continuity pressure point.
- That accepted exact-pocket result does not globally settle
  `non-cyclic-graph`. The repo-level strategic record still keeps
  `non-cyclic-graph = unknown` at general scope and still requires
  representative family-matrix evidence before the acyclic architecture can
  be kept or revised globally.
- The family-matrix settlement bar comes from the accepted capability
  contract: representative positive families `P1` through `P6` plus
  negative or bounded families `N1` through `N6`, with production-surface
  evidence rather than solver-only wins.
- This roadmap family therefore has two phases inside one control plane:
  first settle whether the inherited acyclic architecture is globally
  sufficient for the intended automatic iso-recursive inference family; then,
  only if that settlement gate keeps `non-cyclic-graph`, continue directly
  into production implementation, hardening, and capability-claim work.
- No repo-level automatic iso-recursive inference claim is lawful before
  accepted production-surface evidence shows representative success for
  `P1` through `P6`, bounded or fail-closed behavior for the required
  negative families, and reconstruction-visible output on the existing
  pipeline surfaces without cyclic search, multi-SCC search, second
  interfaces, or fallback widening.
- If accepted evidence instead shows that a required positive family cannot
  be carried inside the inherited acyclic model, this roadmap must record
  that honestly and reopen the `non-cyclic-graph` revision question rather
  than silently continuing into production implementation.
